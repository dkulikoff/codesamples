Share with your team

Figures makes it easy to share your image files with your team. It’s like the result of an experiment where Dropbox, Powerpoint, and Invision App (look it up) were the reactants.

COMMENT

We’re killing email threads. No longer will you have comments about results via email. Upload, share, discuss - and track progress as you see the power of discussing scientific results in context.

RICH THREADS

We wanted to created a tool that made having a conversation about science easier. We’ve included the ability to add rich links to your papers, @yourcolleagues so they pay attention, and chance to point and click on an image to talk about exactly what you see.

Create Figures With Ease

We noticed that scientists are forced to use professional graphic design tools (Photoshop or Illustrator) to create figures that represent their work. We set out to make it faster and easier to create publication worthy scientific figures by developing a set of tools designed specifically for this use. It’s like Photoshop was made for researchers.

Organize all of your labs figures

Figures are one of the most critical digital artifacts of research. They are repurposed in publications, in grant applications, and more. Using Figures you can organize, share, and archive all of your visual data for your whole lab.

LEGACY DATA

We listened to countless principal investigators describe the frustration of grant writing when they can’t find the original figure file. Most end up using a screenshot of a PDF. This low res image diminshes the value fo your application and hurts the credibility of the figure (it’s also simply harder to discern).

IMAGE FIRST ELECTRONIC LAB NOTEBOOK

Sure, Figures let’s you organize and store images. But we’ve gone a step further and created folders that you can add metadata too (like protocols, instrument notes, or brainstorm). We figured that because images and visual data are the reasons researchers use computers than we should create a digital file system that gives them the ability to link the visualr results with experiments themselves.
